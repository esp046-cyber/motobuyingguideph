Route Planning Essentials
  • Gas strategy: Never let tank go below 25%. Fill up when you see gas stations in remote areas. Carry extra liter in fuel bottle for emergencies.
  • Apps to use: Waze (traffic/route), Google Maps (offline download), Angkas/Grab (check road conditions), Weather PH (forecast)
  • Group riding: Ride in staggered formation. Hand signals for hazards. Slowest rider sets pace. Regroup every 50 km or at gas stops.
  • Emergency kit: First aid, tire repair, tools, flashlight, powerbank, emergency cash (‚Ç±2,000+), photocopies of license/OR-CR.
  • Physical prep: Long rides are exhausting. Take 10-min breaks every 2 hours. Stretch legs, hydrate. Never ride when sleepy!
  • Avoid night riding: On highways, 50% of accidents happen 6 PM-6 AM. Poor visibility, drunk drivers, animal crossings. Plan to arrive before sunset.

Buying Tips for Pinoy Riders
  • Test Ride First: Always test ride before buying. Check comfort, handling, and if your feet can flat on the ground. Visit multiple dealers.
  • Check Maintenance Costs: Ask about service intervals and parts availability. Honda and Yamaha have the best parts network in PH.
  • Consider Fuel Efficiency: With rising gas prices, calculate monthly fuel costs. Aim for bikes with 40+ km/L for daily commute.
  • Insurance is Important: Get at least CTPL (‚Ç±350-650/year). Comprehensive insurance (‚Ç±3,000-8,000/year) protects against theft and accidents.
  • Registration Costs: Budget ‚Ç±3,000-5,000 for first registration including plates, OR/CR, and LTO fees. Renewal is ‚Ç±1,500-2,500 yearly.
  • Dealer vs Second-hand: Brand new offers warranty (1-2 years) but costs more. Second-hand saves 20-40% but check service history carefully.
  • Financing Options: Banks offer 12-36 months terms at 1.5-2% monthly interest. Dealer in-house financing is easier but higher rates (2-3%).
  • Monsoon Preparation: Consider bikes with good ground clearance (150mm+) for flooded streets. Store indoors during typhoon season.
  • Resale Value: Honda, Yamaha, and Suzuki hold value best. Avoid rare brands with limited service centers in your area.
  • Safety Gear Budget: Set aside ‚Ç±5,000-10,000 for helmet (‚Ç±2,000-5,000), gloves (‚Ç±500-1,000), jacket (‚Ç±2,000-4,000). Don't skimp on safety.
